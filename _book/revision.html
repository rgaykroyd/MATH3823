9  Appendix: Revision of vectors and matrices

9.1 Notation

Note that in these notes we use lowercase \(y\) or \(y_i\) to denote both observed values or random variables, which should be clear from the context.

Similarly, although all vectors are column vectors, for ease of writing, we may write simply as a horizontal list. Again, the meaning will be clear from context.

Examples of scalars: \[ x, y, \alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \epsilon \hspace{5mm} \text{or} \hspace{5mm} x_i, y_i, \alpha_i, \beta_j, \gamma_j, \delta_j, \epsilon_i \] Examples of \(n\times 1\) vectors: \[ \mathbf{y}=(y_i)=(y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_n), \mathbf{Y}=(Y_i)=(Y_1, Y_2, \ldots, Y_n), \] \[ \boldsymbol{\epsilon} = (\epsilon_i)=(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2,\ldots, \epsilon_n) \] or as: \[ \mathbf{y}=(y_i)= \begin{bmatrix} y_1\\ y_2\\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{bmatrix}, \mathbf{Y}=(Y_i)= \begin{bmatrix} Y_1\\ Y_2\\ \vdots \\ Y_n \end{bmatrix}, \boldsymbol{\epsilon} = (\epsilon_i)= \begin{bmatrix} \epsilon_1\\ \epsilon_2\\ \vdots \\ \epsilon_n \end{bmatrix} \]

Examples of \(p\times 1\) vectors: \[ \mathbf{x}=(x_j) = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_p), \mathbf{X}=(X_j) = (X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_p), \] \[ \boldsymbol{\beta}=(\beta_j)=(\beta_1, \beta_2, \ldots, \beta_p) \] or as \[ \mathbf{x}=(x_j) = \begin{bmatrix}x_1\\ x_2\\ \vdots\\ x_p\end{bmatrix}, \mathbf{X}=(X_j) = \begin{bmatrix}X_1\\ X_2\\ \vdots\\ X_p\end{bmatrix}, \boldsymbol{\beta}=\begin{bmatrix}\beta_1\\ \beta_2\\ \vdots\\ \beta_p\end{bmatrix}, \]

Examples of \(n\times p\) matrices:

\[ X= (X_{ij})= \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12}& \ \ldots & x_{1p} \\ x_{21} & x_{22}& \ \ldots & x_{2p} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n1} & x_{n2}& \ \ldots & x_{np} \\ \end{pmatrix} \]

9.2 Operations

Examples of addition:

\[ \alpha + \boldsymbol{\beta} = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha +\beta_1 \\ \alpha +\beta_2 \\ \vdots \\ \alpha +\beta_p \\ \end{bmatrix} \hspace{5mm} \mathbf{x} +\boldsymbol{\beta} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 +\beta_1 \\ x_2 +\beta_2 \\ \vdots \\ x_p +\beta_p \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Examples of multiplication:

\[ \alpha \boldsymbol{\beta} = \alpha \times \boldsymbol{\beta} = \alpha \cdot \boldsymbol{\beta} = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha \beta_1 \\ \alpha \beta_2 \\ \vdots \\ \alpha \beta_p \\ \end{bmatrix} \hspace{5mm} \mathbf{x} \cdot \boldsymbol{\beta} = \mathbf{x}^T \boldsymbol{\beta} = x_1\beta_1 + x_2\beta_2 + \dots + x_p\beta_p \] \[ X \boldsymbol{\beta} = \begin{bmatrix} x_{11}\beta_1 + x_{12}\beta_2+ \dots + x_{1p}\beta_p \\ x_{21}\beta_1 + x_{22}\beta_2+ \dots + x_{2p}\beta_p \\ \vdots \\ x_{n1}\beta_1 + x_{n2}\beta_2+ \dots + x_{np}\beta_p \\ \end{bmatrix} \]
